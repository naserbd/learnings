## Atomic Habits — Introduction: My Story (Summary)

### The Story in Brief
James Clear was struck in the face by a baseball bat as a teenager, suffered 
severe brain trauma, and was placed in a medically induced coma. His recovery 
was slow and humbling — he lost his spot on his high school varsity team and 
barely played. Rebuilding his life through small, boring, consistent routines 
(sleep, tidiness, study habits, gym work) eventually turned him into a 
record-setting college athlete and academic honoree. Years later he applied 
the same "small and consistent" philosophy to writing online, which grew from 
zero into a huge platform (newsletter, courses, and eventually this book).

### Core Takeaways
- Big life results rarely come from one dramatic moment — they build up from 
  tiny repeated actions.
- Habits are the compound interest of self-improvement: small, boring 
  routines add up over years.
- Setbacks (getting cut from a team, feeling like an impostor) don't 
  disqualify you — consistency after the setback is what matters.
- The book's core framework: **cue → craving → response → reward**, building 
  on older psychology (Skinner's stimulus-response-reward, and Duhigg's 
  cue-routine-reward) but adding the role of emotions and beliefs.
- The book aims to be a practical "operating manual," not academic theory.
